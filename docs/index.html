The Anti-vaccine chronicles

This is a story about the pernicious claims borne out of a single, discredited scientific paper in 1998. But it’s also a story of how this belief has persisted among a growing number of parents, despite its scientific foundations crumbling in the years following its publication.

We’ll tell these two stories in parallel.

To chronicle the the science, we’ll refer to a number of studies (in pink), as well as to investigative journalism pieces focused on a single researcher: Andrew Wakefield.

To chronicle the belief in the autism-vaccine link I’ll use the New York Times archive to show the articles that mention a link between both these terms (in blue). We use these articles not to cast aspersions on the Times, but to demonstrate the fact that these beliefs have grown sufficiently popular for the paper of record to mention them in growing frequency.

Science etc. is a series of shorter visual stories exploring the unexpected aspects of scientific research and their implications.
